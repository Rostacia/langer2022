About

Dr. Robert M. Langer (BS '45, Ph.D. '52) was the vice president and treasurer of The Badger Company, Inc., a leading world-wide engineering firm eventually acquired by Raytheon, as well as a proud veteran of the U.S. Navy. The idea of the student-organized research symposium was first sparked by Dr. Langer and former CEE chair John Walz, with the first symposium occurring in 2003. The primary objective of the annual event is to provide graduate students in the department a forum in a professional setting to present research to fellow students, faculty, and distinguished guests. The symposium also provides a great opportunity for new and established students to bring new research findings to light, hone presentation skills, and inspire others in the department.

Dr. Langer passed away at the age of 90, on September 4th 2015, after a long illness. He will be missed by all those who had the pleasure of knowing this intelligent and lovely man. His thoughtful donation to Yale University brought about the Robert M. Langer Fund for Chemical and Environmental Engineering, which helps to support programs in the Department of Chemical and Environmental Engineering, as well as providing funds for graduate fellowship assistance, faculty research, and support for visiting professors and lecturers. Our gratitude goes out to Dr. Langer and the lasting impact his generosity has had on our Department.

Keynote Speakers

This year we are excited to have two extraordinary women in engineering doing amazing things in their fields.
Be ready to be inspired.

Kathleen Stebe, Ph.D.
University of Pennsylvania

Kathleen J. Stebe received a B.A. in Economics and a Ph.D. in Chemical Engineering at the City College of New York. In 2008, Professor Stebe joined the faculty at the University of Pennsylvania as the Richer and Elizabeth M. Goodwin Professor of Engineering and Applied Science. From 2008-2012, Professor Stebe served as the department chair of Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering. In July 2012, she assumed the post of Deputy Dean for Research in the School of Engineering and Applied Science. Most recently Kate was inducted into the National Academy of Engineering (NAE), although she was officially selected since 2021. Professor Stebe's research focuses on directed assembly in soft matter.Professor Stebe's research focuses on directed assembly in soft matter. Foci include particles interacting by capillarity at interfaces, and particle assembly in complex fluids, including liquid crystals and lipid bilayers. She is an expert in interfacial flows, with particular emphasis on surfactants and Marangoni effects. Other aspects of her research address dynamic surface tension, rheology of protein laden interfaces, and the design of interfaces and bounding surfaces for biological and materials applications.

Mary E. Tucker, Ph.D.
Yale University

Mary Evelyn Tucker is a Senior Lecturer and Research Scholar at Yale University where she has appointments in the School of the Environment as well as the Divinity School and the Department of Religious Studies. She teaches in the joint MA program in religion and ecology and directs the Yale Forum on Religion and Ecology with her husband, John Grim. She has authored with Grim, Ecology and Religion (Island Press, 2014). In addition, they have created six online courses in Religions and Ecology: Restoring the Earth Community. With Brian Thomas Swimme, Tucker and Grim created a multi-media project Journey of the Universe that includes a book (Yale, 2011), an Emmy Award winning film, a series of podcast Conversations, and free online courses from Yale/Coursera. This project was inspired by the evolutionary ideas of Pierre Teilhard de Chardin. She served as vice president of the American Teilhard Association from 1978-2020. She won the Inspiring Yale Teaching Award in 2015 and has been awarded 5 honorary degrees. With John Grim, she received a Lifetime Achievement Award from the International Society for the Study of Religion, Nature, and Culture.
